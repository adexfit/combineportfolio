Introduction

Sphere Signature is a document signing app designed to simplify the process of signing and managing documents electronically. The app provides a user-friendly interface, secure document storage, and seamless integration with various platforms, making it an ideal solution for businesses and individuals looking to streamline their document signing process.

The Problem

Traditional paper-based document signing processes are time-consuming, prone to errors, and require physical presence. With the increasing demand for remote work and digital transactions, there is a need for a more efficient and secure way to sign and manage documents.

This project lasted 3 months (January, 2024 - March, 2024) , and I was the only product designer who worked on it.

My roles

  1. User Research
  2. Interaction design
  3. Wireframing
  4. Prototyping and Testing

User Research

I conducted market research to understand the current challenges faced by businesses and individuals in managing document signing processes

  • Surveys : I conducted a survey to gather quantitative data on user preferences, current challenges with document signing, and desired features in a document signing app. The survey was distributed to a diverse group of participants, including professionals from various industries and individuals who frequently handle documents.

  • Interviews: In-depth interviews were conducted with a select group of participants to delve deeper into their experiences with document signing processes. The interviews aimed to uncover specific pain points, workflow challenges, and desired improvements in document signing solutions.

  • Competitive Analysis: An analysis of existing document signing apps was conducted to identify strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities for improvement in the current landscape of document signing solutions.


User Pain points

  • Time-Consuming Process: Participants expressed frustration with the time-consuming nature of traditional paper-based document signing processes.

  • Security Concerns: Many users highlighted the need for robust security features to protect sensitive documents and personal information.

  • Remote Work Challenges: With the rise of remote work, users emphasized the importance of being able to sign and manage documents from anywhere.

User Preferences

  • Intuitive Interface: Users expressed a strong preference for an intuitive and easy-to-use interface that simplifies the document signing process.

  • Integration with Cloud Services: Seamless integration with popular cloud storage platforms such as Google Drive and Dropbox was identified as a key feature for convenient document management.

  • Real-Time Notifications: Participants expressed a desire for real-time notifications and status updates on document signing activities to stay informed about the progress.

Information Architecture

With the research findings in hand, I began to develop the information architecture of the app. I created a sitemap that outlined the various features and functions of the app, including borrower profiles, lender profiles, loan listings, and payment processing. I also mapped out the user flows for each feature, ensuring that the app was intuitive and easy to use

Wireframe

One of the key design decisions I made during this phase was to create a dashboard for borrowers and lenders. Also, instead of joining the features of the two dashboards, I separated them like we have on Fiverr (for buyers and sellers). This helps the users to focus on the part of the app that is important to them, thereby reducing cognition load.

Design

With the wireframes finalized, I began the visual design phase of the project. I created a style guide that outlined the app's color scheme, typography, and iconography. I also created high-fidelity mockups of the app, incorporating my visual design choices into the wireframes.

One of the key challenges I faced during this phase was creating a design that was both visually appealing and functional. I wanted the app to be easy on the eyes, but also easy to navigate. To achieve this, we used a simple color palette and clean typography, while also incorporating intuitive iconography and clear calls to action.

User testing

I conducted usability tests with 15 users. The key insights obtained are as follows:

  1. Users appreciated the drag-and-drop feature but suggested clearer feedback during uploads.
  2. Users agreed that the signing process was intuitive but stated that it could benefit from a guided tutorial for first-time users.
  3. Users wanted more visibility on document status (e.g., who has signed, pending actions).

Conclusion

Sphere successfully addresses the needs of users seeking a simple, efficient way to sign documents online. Future iterations will focus on expanding integrations with other productivity tools (e.g., Google Drive, Dropbox) and enhancing user feedback mechanisms to continually improve the experience.
