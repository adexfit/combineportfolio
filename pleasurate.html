Overview

Pleasurate is a real estate mobile app that was designed to help Nigerians who are in search of houses to easily find apartments. It is a personal project I embarked on after I had difficulty securing an apartment in Nigeria. This project lasted for a month and I was the only product designer who worked it.

My roles

  1. User Research
  2. Interaction
  3. Visual design
  4. Prototyping and Testing
  5. Information Architecture

Understanding the problem

Renting an apartment in Nigeria is not only difficult, it is stressful. This is because most agents would only try to make money from you without helping you to find the house of your choice.

They would make you fill unnecessary forms and also ask for registration fees before taking you to apartments where you would never lodge your enemy.

While pondering on this problem, I asked myself some questions. What if we have a mobile app that connects Nigerians directly to landlords where people can easily search for houses of their choice? Won’t that prevent the unnecessary headaches associated with the process of renting an apartment in Nigeria?

Research

So I decided to carry out a qualitative research to understand people’s attitude as regards renting of apartments in Nigeria and the associated problems.

I interviewed 15 tenants and 10 landlords. I always record all the interview sessions (with the consent of participants) so that I can listen to them over and over.

Research findings

  1. Landlords also find it difficult to get tenants to rent their apartments.
  2. Agents are responsible for 70% of the frustrations people face while searching for apartments.
  3. Most users interact more with their phones than PC. This could be the reason for the failure of the real estate websites in Nigeria.

Based on the data obtained from the research, I identified two major group of users(tenants and landlords) and created personas for each of them. I also listed out the user pain-points. I created the user journey map so as to effectively design the experience of the user. I also created the Information architecture to understand how each of the part of the app relates to one another. Figma was used for both the wireframing and prototyping.

User testing

The interactive prototype of the wirefame was tested with 5 users before the high fidelity design was done. Most of the users navigated through the app with ease.

Lessons learned

This lesson sharpened my research skills and made me realize that a solid user research can help in easily uncovering user pain-points.
