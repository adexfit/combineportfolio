Introduction

File management refers to the process of organizing, storing, and retrieving files and documents in an efficient and secure manner. It involves developing a system that allows for easy access to important information while ensuring that sensitive data is protected from unauthorized access.

Large companies find it challenging to organize and manage their files due to the amount of files they generate. In this case study, I will be discussing my UX design process for a file management web application.

Background

My client was a tech company that wanted to develop a web app to manage their files. They wanted an app that would allow them to effectively organize, store and retrieve their files. The app needed to be intuitive, visually appealing, and accessible on desktop and mobile devices.

This project lasted 2 months, and I was the only product designer who worked on it.

My roles

  1. Research
  2. Wireframing
  3. User interface design
  4. Prototyping and Testing

User Research

I conducted extensive user research to identify the pain points and challenges faced by the company in managing their files. I conducted interviews with the team leads to gather data on user behavior and their preferences.

One of the key insights I gained from the research was that users often struggle with finding specific files quickly and efficiently. Additionally, users expressed frustration with the lack of organization and clutter in their file systems. They also wanted a flexible way to control the level of access team members have to files uploaded by team leads.


Armed with this knowledge, I set out to design an app that would address these issues.

Information Architecture

With my research in hand, I began to develop the information architecture of the app. I created a sitemap that outlined the various features and functions of the app, including file organization, search functionality, and file sharing. I also mapped out the user flows for each feature, ensuring that the app was intuitive and easy to use.

Wireframe

Once I had a solid information architecture in place, I began creating wireframes of the app. Wireframes are low-fidelity mockups that outline the basic layout and functionality of an app. I used wireframes to test different design ideas and get feedback from users.

One of the key design decisions I made during this phase was to incorporate a search bar into the app, making it easy for users to quickly find specific files or folders

Design

With the wireframes finalized, I began the visual design phase of the project. I created a style guide that outlined the app's color scheme, typography, and iconography. I also created high-fidelity mockups of the app, incorporating my visual design choices into the wireframes.

I used a simple color palette and clean typography, while also incorporating intuitive iconography and clear calls to action.

Accessibility considerations

I used descriptive and informative page and section titles so that users with low vision and difficulty reading text can see few words and know the purpose of each section.

I did not specify important information by color alone so that users with color-blindness can have a good experience on the website.

I maintained a consistent navigation so that users with cognitive limitations, low vision and intellectual disabilities can easily predict location of links on the page.

User testing

With the app design finalized, I conducted user testing to ensure that it met the needs of users. I recruited a group of users to test the app and provide feedback on its usability and functionality.

One of the key insights I gained from user testing was that users found the dashboard and search bar to be intuitive and easy to use. However, some users struggled with the file sharing process, finding it difficult to understand how to share files with others. Based on this feedback, I made some minor adjustments to the app's user flow, making it more clear and straightforward.

Conclusion

I strongly believe that this app has the potential to revolutionize the way companies manage their files, making file management more accessible and convenient than ever before.
